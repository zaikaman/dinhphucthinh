## Context

Contract review is one of the highest-friction legal workflows: expensive, repetitive, and vulnerable to oversight. Missing one high-risk clause can create real financial and operational exposure.

LegalEagle was built as an AI legal assistant focused on one practical outcome: upload a contract PDF and receive a clear, prioritized, and explainable risk package that legal and business stakeholders can both act on.

## Product Scope

- Automated risk detection for liability, ambiguity, compliance, and asymmetry.
- Color-coded redline PDF output with contextual annotations.
- Jurisdiction-aware analysis covering major legal regions.
- Executive summary pages for non-legal decision makers.
- Cached contract analysis for repeatability and speed.
- Multilingual font support for global document handling.

## Architecture and Analysis Pipeline

LegalEagle uses a layered document intelligence architecture:

1. PDF extraction
- Primary path: PDFPlumber for fast local extraction.
- Fallback path: Adobe Extract API for complex layouts, tables, and multi-column docs.

2. Clause-level risk analysis
- GPT-5-nano-based analysis (via configured endpoint) classifies risky clauses.
- Risks are tagged by severity and category (legal, financial, operational, contractual).

3. Explainability and remediation
- Each finding includes impact explanation and concrete redline suggestions.

4. Composite scoring
- Risk score formula uses weighted components:
  50 percent severity, 30 percent ambiguity, 20 percent compliance.
- This creates a transparent ranking model rather than opaque AI output.

5. Output rendering
- Redline contract PDF with high/medium/low highlight colors.
- Executive pages that summarize portfolio-level risk quickly.

## Executive Reporting Design

I focused heavily on executive readability, not only legal depth. The generated report includes:

- Risk Overview Heatmap.
Visual matrix showing risk intensity across categories and severity bands.

- Top 5 Risks and Action Items.
Prioritized issues with confidence and recommended next steps.

- Scoring Methodology Page.
Explains the scoring formula so teams can trust and audit the result.

This design makes the output usable in legal review, procurement, and leadership discussions.

## Jurisdiction and Internationalization

LegalEagle includes jurisdiction-aware logic for 15+ regions (including US, UK, EU, Germany, France, Singapore, Australia, India, and others). This helps avoid one-size-fits-all legal reasoning.

To support multilingual documents and generated summaries, I bundled Unicode-focused fonts (including CJK, Arabic, Hebrew, and Indic coverage) so output quality would hold in cross-border use cases.

## Engineering Challenges

- Document variability.
Contracts vary widely in formatting quality; hybrid extraction was required for production robustness.

- Explainable AI output.
Legal users need traceable reasoning, so I avoided black-box style summaries and leaned into explicit scoring and annotation structure.

- Runtime constraints.
The system was built to operate in constrained hackathon widget compute environments while still producing rich PDF outputs.

## Impact

LegalEagle reduced contract triage friction by translating dense legal text into a structured decision artifact: where the risks are, how severe they are, and what to do next.

## Result

LegalEagle won **first place in the Abysshub Hackathon**. It proved that legal AI can be both technically advanced and operationally useful when explainability, jurisdiction context, and report design are treated as first-class requirements.
