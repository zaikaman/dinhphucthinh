## Context

Most perpetuals users still trade manually, which creates emotional decisions and inconsistent execution.

ClashX was built to shift users from manual trading into bot operations with clear strategy rules, delegated execution, and transparent monitoring on Pacifica Perpetuals.

## Product Scope

- Visual bot builder with graph-based strategy composition.
- AI copilot that can generate and refine trading strategies from natural language.
- Runtime engine for live bot execution with risk controls and lifecycle management.
- Copy trading, bot cloning, and social discovery for strategy distribution.
- Portfolio baskets with allocation and rebalancing logic.
- Backtesting lab for historical validation before live deployment.
- Telegram integration for alerts and portfolio visibility.

## Core Architecture

ClashX was implemented as a full-stack platform:

- Next.js + TypeScript frontend for builder, dashboards, leaderboard, and marketplace UI.
- FastAPI backend for bot management, strategy services, execution orchestration, and APIs.
- Background workers for runtime execution, copy trading, snapshots, portfolio allocation, and backtesting.
- Supabase PostgreSQL for strategy definitions, execution events, metrics, and social data.
- Pacifica REST and WebSocket integrations for market data and order workflows.

## Key Engineering Decisions

- Delegated wallet authorization.
Execution rights are delegated without custody so users keep control of funds while enabling automation.

- Validation-first strategy system.
The builder validates graph structure, required risk settings, and action paths before deployment.

- Reliability under realtime load.
Workers include idempotent execution claims and detailed event logs to prevent duplicate orders and improve auditability.

- Product depth over demo polish.
The scope included marketplace, trust scoring, and portfolio automation to deliver an operational product, not a single feature prototype.

## Hackathon Impact

ClashX combined four track dimensions in one platform: trading automation, analytics, social mechanics, and DeFi composability.

The project proved that Pacifica infrastructure can support a full autonomous trading product with both beginner-friendly UX and advanced strategy controls.

## Result

ClashX won the **Grand Prize (Best Overall)** and the **Trading Applications & Bots Track Prize** at the Pacifica Hackathon, becoming my 6th hackathon win.
