## Context

Manual crypto analysis breaks down under market velocity. Data is fragmented across exchanges, social channels, on-chain activity, and derivatives positioning.

RogueAgent was built as an autonomous swarm architecture that continuously scans, analyzes, scores, and publishes high-conviction opportunities while also supporting automated execution flows.

## Product Scope

- Multi-agent architecture covering scanner, analyzer, writer, publisher, monitor, and futures execution roles.
- Data fusion from market feeds, social/news context, and on-chain indicators.
- Bias-first scanning and strict quality gates for signal discipline.
- Tiered distribution across private and public channels.
- Hyperliquid futures support for testnet and mainnet execution modes.

## Swarm Architecture

RogueAgent is organized around a coordinated pipeline:

1. Orchestrator
Schedules runs, prevents overlapping cycles, manages quotas, and routes data to specialist agents.

2. Scanner
Uses a bias-first methodology (LONG, SHORT, or NEUTRAL) before selecting candidate markets.

3. Analyzer
Applies technical, sentiment, and context scoring to convert candidates into structured signals.

4. Writer and Publisher
Transforms outputs into distribution-ready messaging with tiered timing across channels.

5. Signal Monitor
Tracks active and pending signals, updates status, and triggers TP/SL style lifecycle events.

## Signal Quality Methodology

I designed RogueAgent to reject weak setups by default.

- Bias-first selection to avoid fighting macro direction.
- Confidence and confluence thresholds for publication.
- Risk/reward and stop-loss constraints enforced before promotion.
- Duplicate and recency checks to avoid repetitive low-value output.

The system also includes upgraded chart intelligence paths (including generated chart analysis and multi-timeframe context) to improve decision quality.

## Futures Execution Layer

RogueAgent supports autonomous perpetual futures workflows on Hyperliquid.

- Mainnet mode for live execution and testnet mode for safe iteration.
- Market and conditional-style entry pathways.
- Position management with stop-loss and take-profit logic.
- Signal monitor loop for state transitions and execution lifecycle handling.

This moves the platform from "analysis-only" into a true research-to-execution system.

## ADK-TS and Tooling Strategy

RogueAgent was implemented with ADK-TS patterns for agent construction and structured outputs.

- AgentBuilder pattern for composable specialist agents.
- Tool-driven integration with price, TA, database, and publishing services.
- Schema-driven outputs to keep cross-agent communication deterministic.

This architecture was important for stability and for evolving the platform quickly.

## Operator Experience

I also treated observability as a product requirement:

- Dashboard-first real-time visibility into runs, logs, and signals.
- Query interfaces (including conversational paths) for operational debugging and market exploration.
- Tier-aware distribution that supports premium and public information flows.

## Impact

RogueAgent proved that a disciplined multi-agent design can produce a practical autonomous intelligence stack for volatile markets without sacrificing risk controls.

## Result

RogueAgent won **first place in the Agent Arena Hackathon** and demonstrated a full-cycle approach from market intelligence to actionable and executable trade workflows.
